The Problem

When a borrower fails to repay a loan, the lender absorbs the full loss. At scale, even a small improvement in identifying high-risk applicants translates to significant savings.

Most borrowers repay, but the rare defaulters are exactly who lenders need to identify. A model that flags everyone as low risk achieves over 90% accuracy while catching zero defaulters.

The real question is not ‘Is this model accurate?’ but rather ‘Does it catch the defaulters that matter, without rejecting too many creditworthy borrowers?’

8.2% Default rate in dataset
91.8% Accuracy of a model that never flags anyone
0% Recall of that same model

The Data & Model

Dataset: 291,643 real loan records from Home Credit, a consumer finance company. Originally released as a Kaggle competition.

Features: Derived from each loan’s payment history.

Fraction Late Mean Days Late Max Days Late Pay Ratio Fraction On Time Log Instalment Log Payment Loan Amount Annual Income

Model: Logistic regression - Interpretable, auditable, and consistent with real-world credit scoring practice where regulatory scrutiny favors explainable models.

0.605 AUC on held-out test set
70/30 Train / test split
0.10 Default decision threshold

What’s Next

Current limitations

  • AUC of 0.605 reflects a small feature subset. Full feature models reach ~0.78-0.80.
  • No fairness audit performed. Fair lending law requires group-level disparity analysis before any real deployment.
  • Static model with no retraining pipeline or drift monitoring.

Next steps

  • Full feature set from all Home Credit data sources.
  • Champion / challenger model comparison framework.
  • Automated retraining and performance monitoring.
  • Fairness audit across protected characteristics.
